Random generators study

We use different random generators to get coords of dots that we plot in 256x256 squares.

Linear congruential method

Starting with an arbitrary seed X0, we compute the next random vallue with this formula: Xn+1=(aXn+c)%m

Let m be 2^32. Our random generator will have a period of m if and only if:

  • c is relatively prime to m. (c must be odd.)
  • a - 1 is a multiple of every prime dividing m. (a must be odd)
  • If m is a multiple of 4, a - 1 is a multiple of 4. (a = 4.k + 1)

Mitchell and Moore method

For any n>54, we apply this formula: Xn=(Xn-24+Xn-55)%m

The seed is made of the 55 first element of Xn. The only constraint is that there must be at least one odd number. Otherwise, all the generated numbers will be even.
